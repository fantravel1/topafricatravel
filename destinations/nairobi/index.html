Nairobi: Africa's Most Dynamic Capital

A vibrant metropolis where urban energy, wildlife, innovation, and culture collide in spectacular fashion.

Wildlife in the City

Nairobi is the only capital city in the world with a national park within its boundaries. Nairobi National Park sits just 7 kilometers from the city center, home to lions, rhinos, buffalos, giraffes, and over 400 bird species. The iconic image of wildlife roaming with city skyscrapers behind them is uniquely Nairobi. Add the Giraffe Centre and David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage for a wildlife experience unlike anywhere else.

Silicon Savannah

Nairobi is the beating heart of African tech innovation. Known as Silicon Savannah, the city is home to the iHub, Google's African headquarters, and hundreds of startups transforming the continent. Kenya pioneered mobile money with M-Pesa, and Nairobi's tech ecosystem attracts entrepreneurs and digital nomads from around the world. The energy of innovation is palpable in neighborhoods like Westlands and Kilimani.

Cultural Melting Pot

With over 40 ethnic groups represented, Nairobi is a cultural kaleidoscope. The Nairobi National Museum showcases Kenya's rich heritage. The Karen Blixen Museum tells the story of colonial-era Kenya. The vibrant Maasai Market rotates locations throughout the week, offering handcrafted jewelry, textiles, and art. Contemporary galleries and music venues pulse with creative energy.

Gateway to Safari

Nairobi is the primary gateway to Kenya's world-famous safari destinations. The Maasai Mara, Amboseli, Tsavo, and Lake Nakuru are all accessible by short domestic flights or scenic drives. Most safari operators are based in Nairobi, and the city's Wilson Airport is the hub for bush flights across East Africa.

Things to Do in Nairobi

From urban safaris to rooftop cocktails, Nairobi offers experiences you will not find anywhere else.

🦁

Nairobi National Park

Go on safari just minutes from downtown. Spot lions, rhinos, buffalos, and giraffes against the iconic Nairobi skyline. Morning game drives offer the best wildlife sightings.

🏛

Karen Blixen Museum

Visit the farmhouse made famous by the film "Out of Africa." Explore Karen Blixen's colonial-era home, set in the leafy Karen suburb with views of the Ngong Hills.

🦒

Giraffe Centre

Hand-feed endangered Rothschild's giraffes at eye level from a raised platform. This conservation center in Langata is one of Nairobi's most beloved attractions.

🌳

Karura Forest

Escape into 1,000 hectares of indigenous forest in the heart of the city. Hike, bike, or run along trails past waterfalls, caves, and abundant birdlife in this urban oasis.

🍽

Nairobi Food Scene

From the legendary nyama choma (grilled meat) at Carnivore restaurant to Ethiopian injera in Eastleigh, Nairobi's food scene is Africa's most diverse and exciting.

🎶

Nightlife & Music

Experience Nairobi's legendary nightlife in Westlands and Kilimani. Live music venues feature Kenyan Benga, Afrobeats, and international acts. Rooftop bars offer skyline views.

🐘

David Sheldrick Elephant Trust

Visit orphaned baby elephants during the daily 11am feeding at this world-renowned conservation center. Adopt an elephant and follow their journey back to the wild.

💻

Silicon Savannah Tour

Explore Africa's tech capital. Visit iHub, meet startup founders, and discover how Nairobi is driving innovation from mobile money to renewable energy across the continent.

Getting There

Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO) is East Africa's busiest hub, served by Kenya Airways, Emirates, Ethiopian Airlines, British Airways, KLM, and many others. E-visas are required for most nationalities. The airport is 15 kilometers from the city center, reachable by taxi (30-60 minutes depending on traffic) or the Nairobi Expressway. Wilson Airport handles domestic and bush flights to safari destinations.

Best Time to Visit

Nairobi's elevation (1,795m) gives it a pleasant year-round climate of 12-26°C. The dry seasons (January-March and July-October) are best for wildlife viewing. The long rains (April-June) can make roads muddy but bring beautiful green landscapes. The short rains (November-December) are usually brief afternoon showers that rarely disrupt travel plans.

Money & Costs

Nairobi offers good value. Budget travelers can manage on $30-50 per day in hostels and local restaurants. Mid-range hotel stays and dining run $70-150 per day. Luxury hotels and safari lodges start at $200+. M-Pesa mobile money is used everywhere, and most establishments accept credit cards. The Nairobi Expressway has toll payments via M-Pesa or card.

Local Tips

Use Uber or Bolt for safe, metered transport. Carry a light jacket for cool evenings. Visit Nairobi National Park at dawn for the best wildlife sightings. Book the David Sheldrick visit in advance. Bring layers as Nairobi's weather can shift quickly. The Maasai Market rotates locations daily. Friday and Saturday nights are best for experiencing the vibrant nightlife scene.
