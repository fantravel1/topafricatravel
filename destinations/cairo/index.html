Cairo: The Mother of the World

Africa's largest city where ancient pharaonic wonders coexist with vibrant modern urban life.

The Pyramids & the Sphinx

The Great Pyramids of Giza are the last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World and Egypt's most iconic sight. Built over 4,500 years ago, these monumental tombs continue to inspire awe. The enigmatic Great Sphinx guards the plateau, and the new Grand Egyptian Museum nearby showcases the full splendor of pharaonic civilization, including King Tutankhamun's complete treasure collection.

Living History

Cairo is a living museum spanning millennia. Coptic Cairo preserves some of the world's oldest churches, including the Hanging Church. Islamic Cairo features stunning medieval mosques, madrasas, and the imposing Citadel of Saladin. The Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square holds over 120,000 artifacts. Every neighborhood tells a different chapter of this extraordinary 5,000-year story.

The Eternal Nile

The Nile River has been Cairo's lifeline for millennia. A felucca sailboat ride at sunset offers magical views of the city skyline. Dinner cruises combine Egyptian cuisine with entertainment. The Nile Corniche promenade is perfect for evening walks. For the ultimate experience, multi-day Nile cruises connect Cairo to the temples of Luxor and Aswan.

A City That Never Sleeps

Cairo pulses with energy from dawn to well past midnight. The chaotic charm of its streets, the call to prayer echoing across rooftops, the aroma of koshari and ful medames from street vendors, and the warmth of Egyptian hospitality create an intoxicating urban experience unlike anywhere else on the continent.

Things to Do in Cairo

From ancient wonders to vibrant bazaars, Cairo offers an unmatched depth of history and culture.

🛣

Pyramids of Giza

Stand before the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure. Visit the Great Sphinx and explore the Solar Boat Museum at these 4,500-year-old marvels.

🏛

Egyptian Museum & GEM

Explore the world's greatest collection of pharaonic antiquities. The Grand Egyptian Museum near Giza houses over 100,000 artifacts including Tutankhamun's complete treasure.

🛒

Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

Navigate the narrow alleys of this 14th-century bazaar. Shop for gold, silver, spices, perfumes, lanterns, and papyrus while sipping Turkish coffee at the historic El Fishawi cafe.

⛪

Coptic Cairo

Discover early Christianity at the Hanging Church, the Church of St. Sergius (where the Holy Family reportedly sheltered), the Coptic Museum, and the ancient Ben Ezra Synagogue.

🕐

Islamic Cairo

Explore the medieval heart of the city with its stunning mosques, including Al-Azhar and Sultan Hassan. Visit the Citadel of Saladin for panoramic views over the sprawling metropolis.

⛵

Nile River Experiences

Sail a traditional felucca at sunset, enjoy a dinner cruise with live entertainment, or stroll the Corniche promenade as Cairo's skyline glitters along the water.

🍽

Cairo Food Scene

Feast on koshari (Egypt's national dish), ful medames, shawarma, and fresh-baked aish baladi. Explore street food in Downtown or dine at rooftop restaurants overlooking the Nile.

🌆

Saqqara & Memphis

Day trip to the ancient necropolis of Saqqara to see the Step Pyramid of Djoser, Egypt's oldest pyramid, and the ruins of Memphis, the first capital of unified Egypt.

Getting There

Cairo International Airport (CAI) is Africa's busiest airport and a major hub connecting to every continent. Airlines including EgyptAir, Emirates, Turkish Airlines, Lufthansa, and British Airways serve Cairo. E-visas are available for many nationalities. The airport is connected to the city center by taxi (30-45 minutes), ride-hailing apps, and the Cairo Metro.

Best Time to Visit

October to April offers the most comfortable sightseeing weather. Winter (December-February) is peak season with mild temperatures of 15-22°C. Spring and autumn are warm but pleasant. Avoid June to September when temperatures regularly exceed 40°C. Ramadan affects restaurant hours but offers unique cultural experiences for those visiting during the holy month.

Money & Costs

Cairo is very affordable for international travelers. Budget travelers can manage on $25-40 per day with hostel stays and street food. Mid-range hotels and restaurants run $60-120 per day. Luxury Nile-view hotels start at $150+. Entrance fees to major sites like the Pyramids and museums are reasonable. Tipping (baksheesh) is a cultural norm and expected for many services.

Local Tips

Use ride-hailing apps (Uber/Careem) to avoid taxi price negotiations. Carry small bills for tips. Dress modestly when visiting mosques and churches. Cairo traffic is legendary, so allow extra travel time. The Cairo Metro is efficient for crossing the city. Avoid touts at tourist sites by having your own guide or a firm "la shukran" (no thank you). Drink bottled water only.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cairo

Answers to the most common questions about visiting Cairo.

The best time to visit Cairo is from October to April when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for sightseeing, ranging from 15-25°C. The peak tourist season is December to February. Summer months (June to September) bring intense heat with temperatures often exceeding 40°C, making outdoor exploration of the pyramids and other sites very challenging. Winter evenings can be cool, so bring a light jacket.

The Pyramids of Giza are located on the southwestern outskirts of Cairo, about 18 kilometers from downtown. You can reach them by taxi or ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem (20-40 minutes depending on traffic), by organized tour, or by public bus. The new Grand Egyptian Museum near the pyramids is accessible via the Cairo Metro's extended line. Many hotels arrange private transfers. Traffic in Cairo can be heavy, so allow extra time for travel.

Cairo is generally safe for tourists, with major tourist sites well-guarded by security forces. The areas around the pyramids, Egyptian Museum, and major hotels are heavily patrolled. Standard travel precautions apply: be aware of petty theft in crowded areas like Khan el-Khalili, negotiate prices before using taxis or purchasing goods, and be cautious with unofficial tour guides. Women travelers should dress conservatively. The tourist police are present at all major sites and can be helpful.

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the world's largest archaeological museum, located near the Pyramids of Giza. It houses over 100,000 artifacts spanning 5,000 years of Egyptian history, including the complete Tutankhamun collection with over 5,000 items. The museum features state-of-the-art exhibition halls, conservation laboratories, and stunning views of the pyramids. It is now Cairo's premier cultural attraction and a must-visit alongside the pyramids themselves.
