Serengeti: The Ultimate African Safari

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's most celebrated wildlife destination.

The Great Migration

The Great Migration is the largest mass movement of land animals on Earth. Over two million wildebeest, accompanied by hundreds of thousands of zebras and gazelles, follow an ancient clockwise circuit between the Serengeti and Kenya's Maasai Mara. The dramatic river crossings at the Mara and Grumeti rivers, where crocodiles lie in wait, are among nature's most thrilling spectacles.

Big Five & Beyond

The Serengeti is one of the best places in Africa to see the Big Five: lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo, and rhinoceros. The park is home to the largest lion population on the continent, with an estimated 3,000 individuals. Cheetahs hunt on the open plains, leopards rest in sausage trees, and over 500 bird species fill the skies from martial eagles to lilac-breasted rollers.

Diverse Ecosystems

The Serengeti's 14,763 square kilometers encompass remarkably diverse landscapes. The central Seronera Valley offers year-round game viewing with its riverine woodlands. The Western Corridor features the dramatic Grumeti River crossings. The northern Serengeti hosts the Mara River crossings. The southern Ndutu Plains come alive during calving season, creating a constantly changing safari experience.

Unforgettable Experiences

Beyond game drives, the Serengeti offers extraordinary experiences. Float silently over the plains at dawn in a hot air balloon. Stay in exclusive mobile camps that follow the migration herds. Walk with Maasai guides on foot safaris. Fall asleep to the sound of lions roaring under a canopy of stars. The Serengeti delivers moments that will stay with you forever.

Serengeti Safari Experiences

From dawn game drives to balloon safaris, the Serengeti offers Africa's most extraordinary wildlife encounters.

🦛

Great Migration

Witness two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles on their epic annual journey. Watch dramatic river crossings where herds brave crocodile-infested waters in a primal spectacle.

🦁

Big Five Game Drives

Track lions, leopards, elephants, buffalos, and rhinos with expert guides. Dawn and dusk drives in the central Seronera Valley offer the highest concentration of predators in Africa.

🎈

Hot Air Balloon Safari

Float silently over the endless plains at dawn as wildlife stirs below. Champagne breakfast in the bush follows this once-in-a-lifetime aerial perspective of the Serengeti.

🌎

Seronera Valley

Explore the park's wildlife heartland where the Seronera River attracts year-round concentrations of predators and prey. Leopards drape from kopje rocks and lions rule the grasslands.

💦

Grumeti River Crossings

In May and June, migration herds cross the crocodile-rich Grumeti River in the Western Corridor. The dramatic crossings are less crowded than the Mara River but equally thrilling.

🌃

Ndutu Calving Season

Visit the southern plains from January to March to witness thousands of wildebeest calves born daily. Predators gather for easy prey, creating Africa's most intense wildlife theater.

🚶

Walking Safaris

Experience the bush on foot with Maasai guides. Walking safaris offer an intimate perspective on the Serengeti's smaller wonders: tracks, dung beetles, medicinal plants, and birdsong.

🌅

Sundowners & Stargazing

Sip cocktails as the sun sets over the infinite plains. After dark, the Serengeti sky reveals one of the clearest starscapes on Earth, far from any light pollution.

Getting There

Most visitors fly into Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO) or Julius Nyerere Airport in Dar es Salaam, then take a connecting bush flight to one of the Serengeti's airstrips (Seronera, Grumeti, Kogatende, or Ndutu). Bush flights from Arusha take approximately 1.5 hours. Overland access from Arusha takes 8-10 hours via the Ngorongoro Crater route, which many visitors include as part of a northern Tanzania safari circuit.

Best Time to Visit

The dry season (June-October) is peak safari season with excellent game viewing as animals concentrate around water sources. July-October is prime time for the dramatic Mara River crossings. January-March is calving season on the southern plains. The green season (April-May and November) offers lower prices, fewer crowds, and lush scenery with migratory birds, though some roads may be challenging.

Money & Costs

Serengeti safaris are a significant investment. Budget camping safaris start at $200-350 per person per day. Mid-range tented camps run $400-800 per day. Luxury lodges range from $1,000-2,500+ per person per night all-inclusive. Park fees are $70.80 per adult per 24 hours. Balloon safaris cost approximately $500-600. Most safari packages are all-inclusive with meals, game drives, and park fees. Tipping guides $15-25 per day is customary.

Safari Tips

Book well in advance for peak season (July-October). Bring binoculars, a good camera with zoom lens, neutral-colored clothing, sunscreen, and a warm layer for early morning drives. Listen to your guide's expertise. Patience is key: the best sightings often come when you wait. Choose accommodation near where the migration herds are expected. Consider combining the Serengeti with Ngorongoro Crater for a complete northern Tanzania experience.

Frequently Asked Questions About the Serengeti

Answers to the most common questions about planning a Serengeti safari.

The Serengeti offers excellent wildlife viewing year-round, but the best time depends on what you want to see. For the Great Migration river crossings at the Mara River, visit from July to October. The calving season on the Ndutu Plains (January-March) is spectacular with thousands of baby wildebeest born daily. The dry season (June-October) concentrates wildlife around water sources, making sightings easier. The green season (November-May) offers lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and lower prices.

Serengeti safari costs vary widely based on accommodation type and season. Budget camping safaris start from $200-350 per person per day including park fees, transport, meals, and guide. Mid-range tented camps and lodges run $400-800 per person per day. Luxury lodges and exclusive mobile camps during peak migration season can range from $1,000-2,500+ per person per night all-inclusive. Park entrance fees are $70.80 per adult per 24 hours. Hot air balloon safaris cost approximately $500-600 per person.

The Great Migration is the largest mass movement of land animals on Earth, involving over two million wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles moving in a continuous clockwise circuit between Tanzania's Serengeti and Kenya's Maasai Mara. The migration follows the rains and fresh grazing. From December to March, herds are in the southern Serengeti (Ndutu) for calving. April to June they move through the Western Corridor and Grumeti. July to October they cross the Mara River into Kenya. November they begin heading south again.

The Serengeti offers diverse accommodation ranging from budget to ultra-luxury. Options include public campsites (basic, affordable), private tented camps (comfortable, mid-range), permanent luxury lodges with swimming pools and fine dining, exclusive mobile camps that follow the migration herds, and ultra-luxury properties with private guides and butlers. Popular choices include Seronera Wildlife Lodge in the central Serengeti, Four Seasons Safari Lodge, and mobile camps along the migration route. Booking well in advance is essential, especially for peak season (July-October).
