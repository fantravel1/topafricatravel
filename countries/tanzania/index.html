From the endless plains of the Serengeti to the summit of Africa's highest peak, from the spice-scented shores of Zanzibar to the ancient caldera of Ngorongoro — Tanzania is where Africa reveals its grandest wonders.

Discover Tanzania

East Africa's crown jewel, home to some of the most iconic landscapes and wildlife on the planet.

The Serengeti

The Serengeti ecosystem spans 30,000 square kilometers of grassland, savanna, and woodland. It hosts the Great Migration, where over two million wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle traverse the plains in an annual cycle that ranks among the greatest natural spectacles on Earth.

Mount Kilimanjaro

Rising to 5,895 meters, Kilimanjaro is Africa's tallest peak and the world's highest free-standing mountain. Its snow-capped summit, five distinct climate zones, and accessibility to non-technical climbers make it one of the planet's most sought-after trekking destinations.

Zanzibar Archipelago

The semi-autonomous island of Zanzibar is a tropical paradise steeped in centuries of Swahili, Arab, Persian, and European influence. White-sand beaches, turquoise waters, coral reefs, spice plantations, and the UNESCO-listed Stone Town create an enchanting coastal experience.

Natural Wonders

Beyond its headline attractions, Tanzania shelters the Ngorongoro Crater — the world's largest intact volcanic caldera — Lake Manyara's flamingo-lined shores, the remote wilderness of the Selous (now Nyerere National Park), and the ancient baobab-studded landscapes of Tarangire.

Top Destinations in Tanzania

Seven iconic places that make Tanzania one of Africa's most extraordinary countries to explore.

🦁

Serengeti National Park

Africa's most celebrated safari destination. Witness the Great Migration, track the Big Five, and experience sunsets across endless golden plains teeming with wildlife year-round.

🏖

Zanzibar

A tropical archipelago of pristine beaches, vibrant coral reefs, aromatic spice farms, and the historic Stone Town. The perfect complement to a mainland safari adventure.

⛰

Mount Kilimanjaro

Africa's rooftop at 5,895 meters. Choose from seven trekking routes through rainforest, moorland, alpine desert, and glacial summit. A bucket-list achievement for adventurers worldwide.

🌍

Ngorongoro Crater

The world's largest intact volcanic caldera shelters over 25,000 animals in a 260-square-kilometer natural amphitheater. Spot the Big Five in a single game drive within this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

🗻

Stone Town

Zanzibar's historic heart and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Wander centuries-old coral stone alleyways, ornate carved doors, bustling bazaars, and the atmospheric Forodhani Gardens night food market.

🌴

Nyerere National Park (Selous)

One of Africa's largest protected areas, offering uncrowded boat safaris along the Rufiji River, walking safaris, and extraordinary concentrations of elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and wild dogs.

🌴

Lake Manyara National Park

A compact gem on the northern safari circuit. Famous for tree-climbing lions, vast flocks of flamingos on the alkaline lake, lush groundwater forest, and panoramic Rift Valley escarpment views.

Best Experiences in Tanzania

Unforgettable adventures that make Tanzania one of the world's top travel destinations.

🦁

The Great Migration

Witness two million wildebeest and zebra crossing the Serengeti in the greatest wildlife spectacle on Earth. River crossings between July and September are the dramatic highlight.

⛰

Climb Mount Kilimanjaro

Summit Africa's highest peak through five climate zones. Routes like Machame and Lemosho offer 6-9 day treks with staggering scenery from tropical jungle to glacial ice fields.

🏖

Zanzibar Beach Escape

Relax on powdery white sands at Nungwi, Paje, or Matemwe. Snorkel vibrant coral reefs, dive at Mnemba Atoll, kitesurf in warm Indian Ocean trade winds, or sail on a traditional dhow at sunset.

🍽

Zanzibar Spice Tours

Explore fragrant spice plantations where cloves, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and black pepper grow. Learn about Zanzibar's centuries-old spice trade that shaped its history and culture.

🎸

Maasai Cultural Visits

Engage respectfully with Maasai communities near the Serengeti and Ngorongoro. Learn about pastoralist traditions, beadwork, storytelling, and a way of life sustained for centuries across East Africa.

Visa & Entry

Most visitors require a visa. E-visas are available online for many nationalities. Visa on arrival is offered at major airports (Dar es Salaam, Kilimanjaro). Tourist visas cost $50 USD (single entry) or $100 USD for US citizens (multiple entry). Valid passport with at least 6 months remaining is required. Yellow fever vaccination certificate may be required if arriving from an endemic country.

Currency & Costs

The Tanzanian Shilling (TZS) is the local currency. US dollars are widely accepted for tourism services, park fees, and accommodation. ATMs are available in major cities and tourist areas. Budget safaris start around $150-250/day, mid-range $300-600/day, and luxury lodges from $700-2,000+/day. Zanzibar accommodations range from $30/night budget to $500+/night luxury resorts.

Language & Communication

Swahili (Kiswahili) and English are the official languages. Swahili is the lingua franca spoken by nearly all Tanzanians. English is widely used in tourism, business, and education. Learning a few Swahili phrases — Jambo (hello), Karibu (welcome), Asante (thank you) — is deeply appreciated by locals and enriches the travel experience.

Getting Around

Domestic flights connect major destinations (Arusha, Serengeti, Zanzibar, Dar es Salaam). The northern safari circuit is well-connected by road. Ferries run between Dar es Salaam and Zanzibar (approximately 2 hours). Most safaris are conducted in 4x4 vehicles with experienced driver-guides. Reliable tour operators handle logistics for safari itineraries.

Health & Safety

Consult a travel health specialist before visiting. Antimalarial medication is recommended. Drink bottled or purified water. Tanzania is generally safe for tourists, particularly on organized safaris and in Zanzibar. Use reputable, licensed tour operators. Travel insurance covering medical evacuation is strongly recommended for safari and Kilimanjaro treks.

Best Times to Visit

June to October (dry season) is ideal for safari, with excellent game viewing as animals gather at water sources. July to September offers Great Migration river crossings. December to March is perfect for Zanzibar's beaches and Kilimanjaro climbs. The short rains (November) and long rains (March-May) bring lower prices and fewer crowds, with green season photography opportunities.

Culture & Heritage

Tanzania is home to over 120 ethnic groups living in remarkable harmony — a tapestry of traditions that enriches every visit.

A Mosaic of Cultures

Tanzania's 120+ ethnic groups — including the Maasai, Sukuma, Chagga, Hadzabe, and Swahili communities — each bring distinct languages, traditions, and artistry. This diversity is united by a strong national identity and the Swahili language, creating one of Africa's most harmonious multicultural societies.

Swahili Heritage

The Swahili coast has been a crossroads of African, Arab, Persian, Indian, and European cultures for over a millennium. Stone Town's architecture, Zanzibar's cuisine, and the Swahili language itself reflect centuries of maritime trade and cultural exchange along the Indian Ocean.

Respectful Travel

When visiting Tanzania, dress modestly in rural areas and on Zanzibar (a predominantly Muslim island). Ask permission before photographing people. Support community-based tourism initiatives that benefit local populations directly. Engage with Maasai and other communities through authorized, respectful cultural programs rather than informal encounters.

Frequently Asked Questions About Traveling to Tanzania

Essential answers to help you plan the perfect Tanzanian adventure.

The best time to visit Tanzania for safari is during the dry season from June to October, when wildlife congregates around water sources and vegetation is thinner, making animals easier to spot. For Zanzibar beaches, December to March offers the warmest and driest weather. The Great Migration river crossings in the Serengeti typically occur between July and September.

Most visitors to Tanzania require a visa. Many nationalities can obtain an e-visa online before travel or a visa on arrival at major entry points including Julius Nyerere International Airport (Dar es Salaam) and Kilimanjaro International Airport. A single-entry tourist visa typically costs $50 USD and is valid for up to 90 days. US citizens require a $100 multiple-entry visa. Always check the latest requirements before travel.

Tanzania safari costs vary widely depending on the type of experience. Budget camping safaris start from $150-250 per person per day. Mid-range lodges and tented camps run $300-600 per person per day. Luxury safari lodges and fly-in experiences range from $700 to over $2,000 per person per day. Park fees, transport, and season all affect pricing. The northern circuit (Serengeti, Ngorongoro) tends to be more expensive than the southern circuit.

Tanzania is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. The country has a well-established tourism industry, particularly for safari and Zanzibar beach holidays. As with any travel destination, visitors should exercise standard precautions: use reputable tour operators, avoid walking alone at night in cities, keep valuables secure, and follow local advice. Tanzania's people are known for their warmth and hospitality, and the Swahili greeting 'Karibu' (welcome) reflects the genuine friendliness visitors experience.

A minimum of 7-10 days is recommended to experience Tanzania's highlights. A typical itinerary includes 3-4 days in the Serengeti and Ngorongoro Crater, 3-4 days in Zanzibar, and travel days between destinations. For climbing Mount Kilimanjaro, add 6-9 days depending on the route. To include the southern parks (Nyerere/Selous, Ruaha) or Lake Manyara, plan for 12-14 days. Two to three weeks allows for a comprehensive Tanzanian experience.
