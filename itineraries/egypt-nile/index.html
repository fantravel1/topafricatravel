Day-by-Day Itinerary

Your complete 9-day Egypt adventure broken down day by day.

Day 1: Arrive in Cairo

Arrive at Cairo International Airport and transfer to your hotel in the Zamalek or Garden City district, overlooking the Nile. After settling in, begin exploring the mother of all cities. Visit the Egyptian Museum in Tahrir Square, home to over 120,000 artifacts including the golden treasures of Tutankhamun and the Royal Mummies Room. The sheer density of pharaonic treasures is overwhelming. In the evening, take a felucca ride on the Nile at sunset, watching Cairo's skyline glow in golden light. Welcome dinner at a restaurant overlooking the river with views of the city illuminated at night.

Accommodation: Cairo hotel · Meals: Dinner

Day 2: Cairo – Islamic & Coptic Quarters

Full day exploring Cairo's rich layers of history. Morning in Islamic Cairo: walk through the medieval gates of Bab Zuweila, visit the stunning Mosque of Muhammad Ali perched atop the Citadel of Saladin with panoramic city views, and explore the 10th-century Al-Azhar Mosque and University, the oldest degree-granting institution in the world. Afternoon in Coptic Cairo: visit the Hanging Church (Al-Mu'allaqa), one of the oldest churches in Egypt, the Coptic Museum, and the Ben Ezra Synagogue. End the day in Khan el-Khalili bazaar, Cairo's legendary 14th-century market. Haggle for brass lanterns, perfumes, spices, and papyrus art while sipping strong Egyptian coffee.

Accommodation: Cairo hotel · Meals: Breakfast

Day 3: Giza Pyramids & Saqqara

The day you have been dreaming about. Drive to the Giza Plateau to stand before the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the only surviving Wonder of the Ancient World, built over 4,500 years ago with 2.3 million limestone blocks. Explore the Pyramid of Khafre and the smaller Pyramid of Menkaure. Face the enigmatic Great Sphinx, the oldest monumental sculpture in the world. Optional: enter the Great Pyramid's narrow passages to reach the King's Chamber deep inside. After lunch with views of the pyramids, continue to Saqqara to visit the Step Pyramid of Djoser, the world's first monumental stone structure (c. 2670 BC), and the beautifully painted Mastaba of Ti. Visit the nearby ruins of Memphis, ancient Egypt's first capital.

Accommodation: Cairo hotel · Meals: Breakfast & lunch

Days 4–5: Luxor

Day 4: Fly from Cairo to Luxor (1 hour), ancient Thebes, the greatest open-air museum on Earth. Begin on the East Bank at Karnak Temple Complex, the largest ancient religious site ever constructed. Walk through the Great Hypostyle Hall with its 134 massive columns, each one carved with hieroglyphs. Continue to Luxor Temple, dramatically illuminated at night, connected to Karnak by the recently restored 3-kilometer Avenue of Sphinxes. Evening stroll along the Nile corniche.

Day 5: Cross the Nile to the West Bank, the realm of the dead. Enter the Valley of the Kings where 63 royal tombs were carved deep into the limestone hills, including the tomb of Tutankhamun. The vibrant wall paintings depicting the pharaohs' journey to the afterlife are astonishingly preserved. Visit the Temple of Hatshepsut, Egypt's greatest female pharaoh, carved into the cliffs at Deir el-Bahari. Stop at the Colossi of Memnon, two 18-meter statues guarding the entrance to the vanished mortuary temple of Amenhotep III. Optional hot air balloon ride at dawn over the West Bank.

Accommodation: Luxor hotel or Nile-view guesthouse · Meals: Breakfast daily

Days 6–7: Nile Cruise

Day 6: Board your Nile cruise ship or traditional dahabiya in Luxor and begin sailing south toward Aswan. The gentle rhythm of the Nile sets the pace as you pass palm-fringed banks, sugar cane fields, and timeless mud-brick villages. Stop at the Temple of Edfu, the best-preserved temple in Egypt, dedicated to the falcon god Horus. Its massive pylon gateway and inner sanctum are breathtaking in their completeness. Continue sailing through the afternoon, watching fishermen cast nets and water buffalo bathe at the river's edge.

Day 7: Morning visit to Kom Ombo, a unique double temple dedicated to both the crocodile god Sobek and the falcon god Horus. The temple perches dramatically on a bend in the Nile and houses a small museum with mummified crocodiles. Continue sailing to Aswan, arriving in the late afternoon. Enjoy a felucca ride around Elephantine Island and Kitchener's Island (Aswan Botanical Garden) as the sun sets over the granite boulders and the Aga Khan Mausoleum glows golden on the West Bank.

Accommodation: Nile cruise ship · Meals: All meals included

Day 8: Aswan

A full day exploring Aswan, Egypt's sunniest and most relaxed city. Morning boat trip to the island Temple of Philae, dedicated to the goddess Isis and rescued from rising Nile waters by a UNESCO project that relocated it stone by stone to Agilkia Island. The temple's elegant columns and reliefs are among the most beautiful in Egypt. Visit the Unfinished Obelisk in the ancient granite quarries, an enormous obelisk abandoned after a crack appeared, offering unique insights into ancient stonecutting techniques. Afternoon visit to the Aswan High Dam, the modern engineering marvel that tamed the Nile. Evening at leisure in Aswan's colorful Nubian souk, shopping for spices, henna, and handmade Nubian crafts. Optional evening Nubian dinner and music in a traditional village.

Accommodation: Aswan hotel or cruise ship · Meals: Breakfast

Day 9: Abu Simbel & Departure

Pre-dawn departure for the 3-hour drive (or short flight) south to Abu Simbel, one of Egypt's most awe-inspiring monuments. The Great Temple of Ramesses II features four colossal 20-meter statues of the pharaoh carved directly from the cliff face, guarding an interior with vivid wall reliefs depicting the Battle of Kadesh. Next door, the smaller Temple of Hathor, dedicated to Queen Nefertari, is equally stunning. The entire complex was cut apart and reassembled 65 meters higher in the 1960s to save it from Lake Nasser's rising waters, one of the greatest engineering feats of the 20th century. Return to Aswan for your departure flight via Cairo, carrying the grandeur of ancient Egypt in your heart.

Accommodation: N/A (departure day) · Meals: Breakfast

Practical Information

Essential details for planning your Egypt and Nile adventure.

Getting There & Around

International flights arrive at Cairo International Airport (CAI). Internal flights connect Cairo to Luxor (1 hour) and Aswan (1.5 hours). The Nile cruise covers the Luxor-Aswan segment over 2 days. Abu Simbel is reached by a 3-hour drive from Aswan or a 45-minute flight. Alternatively, overnight sleeper trains connect Cairo to Luxor and Aswan. Within cities, taxis and ride-hailing apps (Uber, Careem) are widely available.

Money & Costs

Egypt's currency is the Egyptian Pound (EGP). Egypt offers excellent value for travelers. Budget travelers can manage on $40-70/day. Mid-range travel runs $100-200/day. Pyramids entry is approximately $13, Valley of the Kings $12, Karnak $10. ATMs are widely available in cities. Credit cards are accepted at hotels, cruise ships, and larger restaurants. Cash is essential for bazaars, taxis, and tips. Tipping (baksheesh) is an integral part of Egyptian culture: carry small bills.

Visa & Entry

Most nationalities require a visa for Egypt. E-visas are available for citizens of 46 countries ($25 single entry, $60 multiple entry) and can be applied for online before travel. Visas on arrival are also available for many nationalities at Egyptian airports. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months. No vaccinations are mandatory, though hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. Egypt is a conservative country; modest dress is respectful, especially when visiting mosques.

What to Pack

Lightweight, breathable clothing in neutral or white colors. A hat, quality sunglasses, and SPF 50+ sunscreen are essential. Comfortable walking shoes for temples and uneven terrain. A warm layer for cool desert evenings and air-conditioned interiors. Modest clothing for mosques: women should carry a scarf to cover shoulders and hair. A good camera and portable charger. Bring hand sanitizer and basic medications as Egyptian pharmacies may not stock familiar brands.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Egypt and Nile itinerary.

The best time to visit Egypt is from October to April when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for sightseeing. Winter months (December-February) are the most pleasant with daytime temperatures around 20-25 degrees Celsius in Upper Egypt. Summer (May-September) brings extreme heat, especially in Luxor and Aswan where temperatures can exceed 45 degrees Celsius. For Nile cruises, the cooler months are ideal. Cairo is pleasant from October to April. If you visit during Ramadan, be aware that some services may have reduced hours.

Nile cruise costs vary widely depending on the vessel and level of luxury. Budget cruises on older boats start from $50-100 per person per night. Mid-range cruises with comfortable cabins and good food run $150-300 per person per night. Luxury dahabiyas (traditional sailing boats) and five-star cruise ships range from $300-800+ per person per night, all-inclusive with guided excursions. Most Nile cruises run between Luxor and Aswan (3-4 nights) or Aswan to Luxor (3-4 nights). Book well in advance during peak season (October-March).

Egypt's major tourist sites are generally safe and heavily guarded. Cairo, the Giza Pyramids, Luxor, Aswan, and Nile cruise routes have strong security presence and tourist police. Use licensed guides and reputable tour operators. Take normal precautions against petty theft in crowded areas. Women should dress modestly outside resort areas. The main tourist circuits covered in this itinerary are well-established and safe. Always check your government's latest travel advisories before departure. Travel insurance is recommended.
