Day-by-Day Itinerary

Your complete 12-day South Africa adventure broken down day by day.

Day 1: Arrive in Cape Town

Arrive at Cape Town International Airport and transfer to your hotel in the City Bowl or Sea Point area. If weather permits, take the revolving cable car up Table Mountain for panoramic views over the city, harbor, and Robben Island. The flat-topped mountain, one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature, offers hiking trails and diverse fynbos flora found nowhere else on Earth. As evening falls, stroll the V&A Waterfront for dinner with views of Table Mountain illuminated against the night sky. Welcome briefing over local craft beer or Cape wine.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel or boutique guesthouse · Meals: Dinner

Day 2: Cape Town – Cape Peninsula

Full-day tour of the Cape Peninsula, one of the world's most scenic coastal drives. Drive along Chapman's Peak with its sheer cliffs and ocean views. Visit Boulders Beach to see the colony of endangered African penguins waddling among granite boulders. Continue to Cape Point, the dramatic headland at the southwestern tip of Africa where the Atlantic and Indian Oceans meet. Explore the Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve with its rich fynbos vegetation. Stop at a seaside village for fish and chips before returning via the charming fishing harbor of Kalk Bay and the colorful houses of Bo-Kaap in Cape Town.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel or boutique guesthouse · Meals: Breakfast

Day 3: Cape Town – Robben Island & Culture

Morning ferry to Robben Island, the UNESCO World Heritage Site where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 of his 27 years. A former political prisoner guides you through the cell blocks and shares personal stories of courage and resistance. Return to the mainland and visit the District Six Museum, chronicling the forced removals during apartheid. Afternoon free to explore the vibrant neighborhoods of Woodstock and Observatory with their street art, vintage shops, and craft coffee scene. Evening food tour through the city's diverse culinary scene featuring Cape Malay, braai, and biltong.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel or boutique guesthouse · Meals: Breakfast

Day 4: Cape Winelands

Drive 45 minutes east to the Cape Winelands, South Africa's premier wine-producing region. Begin in Stellenbosch, a university town with Cape Dutch architecture and oak-lined streets, visiting two or three estates for tastings of Pinotage, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Chenin Blanc. Continue to Franschhoek, the country's culinary capital, founded by French Huguenot settlers in the 17th century. Enjoy a gourmet lunch paired with local wines at one of the valley's acclaimed restaurants. Visit the Huguenot Memorial Museum and explore the charming main street. Afternoon tasting at a boutique estate before returning to your accommodation in the Winelands or continuing east toward the Garden Route.

Accommodation: Winelands estate or guesthouse · Meals: Breakfast & lunch

Days 5–7: Garden Route

Day 5 begins the legendary Garden Route drive east along the N2 highway. Stop in Hermanus (June-November) for shore-based whale watching, one of the best in the world for Southern Right whales. Continue to Mossel Bay or Wilderness, a tranquil beach town set between a lagoon and the Indian Ocean.

Day 6 explores the heart of the Garden Route. Visit Knysna, a charming town on a scenic lagoon framed by sandstone headlands. Take a boat cruise to the Knysna Heads and sample fresh Knysna oysters. In the afternoon, visit the Knysna Elephant Park or Monkeyland Primate Sanctuary. Continue to Plettenberg Bay for spectacular beach walks and ocean views.

Day 7 is dedicated to Tsitsikamma National Park, the crown jewel of the Garden Route. Hike the first section of the famous Otter Trail to the suspension bridge over Storms River Mouth, where ancient forest meets crashing Indian Ocean waves. The park offers kayaking, zip-lining through the forest canopy, and the Big Tree walk among 800-year-old yellowwood trees. Drive or fly to Johannesburg in the evening.

Accommodation: Garden Route lodges and guesthouses · Meals: Breakfast daily

Days 8–9: Johannesburg

Day 8 arrives in Johannesburg, South Africa's vibrant economic hub. Visit the Apartheid Museum, a profoundly moving journey through the country's journey from segregation to democracy. Continue to Soweto for a guided tour of this historic township: see Mandela House on Vilakazi Street (the only street in the world to have housed two Nobel laureates), the Hector Pieterson Memorial, and Regina Mundi Church. Enjoy a traditional lunch at a Soweto restaurant.

Day 9 explores Johannesburg's cultural renaissance. Visit Constitution Hill, a former prison complex now home to South Africa's Constitutional Court. Explore the Maboneng Precinct, a revitalized inner-city neighborhood buzzing with galleries, cafes, and craft markets. Afternoon visit to the Cradle of Humankind UNESCO site where some of humanity's oldest fossils have been discovered. Prepare for the final safari leg of the trip.

Accommodation: Johannesburg hotel · Meals: Breakfast daily, lunch on Day 8

Days 10–12: Kruger National Park

Day 10: Drive or fly to Kruger National Park, one of Africa's largest and most diverse game reserves spanning nearly 2 million hectares. Check into your safari lodge and enjoy an afternoon game drive as the golden light draws animals to water holes. First sightings often include elephants, zebras, giraffes, and impalas. Sundowner drinks in the bush as the African night descends.

Day 11: Full day of game drives with expert rangers. Dawn drive to catch predators returning from the night's hunt: lions, leopards, and hyenas are most active at first light. Midday break at the lodge, then an afternoon drive focusing on the Big Five. Kruger is home to over 147 mammal species and 500 bird species. Optional night drive to spot nocturnal animals like aardvarks, civets, and bush babies.

Day 12: Final early morning bush walk with an armed ranger, experiencing the savanna on foot and learning to read animal tracks and signs. After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your departure flight. Carry the spirit of the Rainbow Nation and the roar of lions into your memories forever.

Accommodation: Kruger safari lodge or bush camp · Meals: All meals included

Practical Information

Essential details for planning your South Africa highlights trip.

Getting There & Around

International flights arrive at Cape Town International Airport (CPT). Departure is from O.R. Tambo International Airport (JNB) in Johannesburg or Kruger Mpumalanga International Airport (MQP). The Garden Route is best driven by rental car (excellent roads, left-hand driving). Internal flights connect Cape Town to Johannesburg (2 hours) and Johannesburg to Kruger (1 hour). Alternatively, drive the entire route for maximum flexibility.

Money & Costs

South Africa's currency is the Rand (ZAR). The country offers excellent value compared to other safari destinations. Budget travelers can manage on $80-120/day. Mid-range options run $150-250/day. Kruger park entrance is approximately $25/day for international visitors. ATMs are widely available. Credit cards are accepted almost everywhere. Tipping: 10-15% in restaurants, ZAR 100-200 per person per game drive for guides and trackers.

Visa & Entry

Citizens of many countries including the US, UK, EU, Canada, and Australia can enter South Africa visa-free for 90 days. Your passport must be valid for 30 days beyond your departure date and have at least 2 blank pages. No vaccinations are mandatory for entry, but hepatitis A and typhoid are recommended. A yellow fever certificate is required if arriving from an endemic country. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for the Kruger area.

What to Pack

South Africa has diverse climates, so layer your clothing. Cape Town can be windy; bring a windbreaker. The Garden Route is mild; bring comfortable walking shoes and rain gear. For Kruger safaris, pack neutral-colored clothing, binoculars, sunscreen, insect repellent, and a warm fleece for early morning game drives. A good camera with zoom lens is essential. South Africa uses Type M power plugs (large 3-pin round). Adapters are available at airports and shops.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the South Africa Highlights itinerary.

South Africa is a year-round destination, but the best time depends on your priorities. For wildlife viewing in Kruger, the dry winter months (May-September) are ideal as animals concentrate around water sources and vegetation is thinner. Cape Town and the Garden Route are best from September to April when weather is warm and sunny. The Winelands are beautiful year-round but harvest season (February-April) is special. This itinerary works best from September to April when you get the best of Cape Town's weather while still enjoying good game viewing.

South Africa's tourist areas are generally safe with sensible precautions. Cape Town's tourist zones (V&A Waterfront, Table Mountain, Winelands, Garden Route) are well-patrolled and safe. In Johannesburg, stick to tourist areas like Sandton, Rosebank, and organized tours of Soweto. Avoid walking alone at night in cities, keep valuables out of sight, and use reputable transport. Safari lodges and national parks are very safe. South Africa has excellent tourist infrastructure and English is widely spoken, making it one of Africa's most accessible destinations.

Renting a car is the best way to experience the Garden Route at your own pace. Roads are excellent, well-signposted, and driving is on the left side (same as UK). The N2 highway along the Garden Route is one of Africa's most scenic drives. Rental cars are affordable by international standards, starting from around $25-40 per day. Alternatively, organized Garden Route tours and the Baz Bus hop-on-hop-off service are available. For Kruger, you can self-drive through the park or join guided safari vehicles.
