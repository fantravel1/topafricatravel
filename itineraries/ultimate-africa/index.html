Day-by-Day Itinerary

Your complete 21-day continent-spanning Africa adventure broken down day by day.

Day 1: Arrive in Marrakech, Morocco

Arrive at Marrakech Menara Airport and transfer to a traditional riad in the medina. After settling in, explore the Bahia Palace with its intricate zellige tilework and painted cedar ceilings. As evening falls, plunge into the sensory overload of Jemaa el-Fna: snake charmers, Gnawa musicians, henna artists, and endless food stalls. Sample Moroccan street food, including lamb tagine, msemen flatbread, and freshly squeezed orange juice. A rooftop dinner overlooking the square sets the stage for the adventure ahead.

Accommodation: Marrakech riad · Meals: Dinner

Day 2: Marrakech Exploration

Full day in the Red City. Morning visit to the Majorelle Garden and Yves Saint Laurent Museum, followed by the Saadian Tombs and Ben Youssef Madrasa. Dive into the labyrinthine souks: haggle for leather goods in the tanners' quarter, admire handwoven carpets, and watch brass artisans at work. Afternoon Moroccan cooking class learning to prepare pastilla, harira, and preserved lemon chicken. Experience a traditional hammam in the evening for the ultimate relaxation.

Accommodation: Marrakech riad · Meals: Breakfast & lunch

Day 3: Atlas Mountains

Drive into the High Atlas Mountains, stopping in the Ourika Valley to visit Berber villages and trek through terraced walnut and almond groves. Lunch in a traditional Berber home perched on a mountainside with panoramic views. Visit the Setti Fatma waterfalls, climbing through the valley alongside local guides. The Atlas Mountains offer a stunning contrast to Marrakech's intensity: crisp mountain air, snow-capped peaks (in season), and the warm hospitality of Berber communities. Return to your mountain lodge or continue toward the Sahara.

Accommodation: Atlas Mountains lodge or kasbah · Meals: Breakfast & lunch

Days 4–5: Sahara Desert

Day 4: Drive over the Tizi n'Tichka Pass and through the dramatic kasbahs of the Dades and Todra Gorges, arriving at Merzouga at the edge of Erg Chebbi. Mount camels for the sunset trek into the golden dunes, reaching your Berber desert camp as stars begin to appear. Traditional dinner with drumming under an immense canopy of stars, far from any light pollution. The silence of the Sahara is profound and transformative.

Day 5: Wake for sunrise over the dunes, a watercolor of pinks, golds, and purples across the sand. After breakfast, depart for Fez via the Ziz Valley, passing palm oases and the cedar forests of the Middle Atlas. Arrive in Fez by evening for a brief exploration of this ancient imperial city. Optional visit to the Chouara Tanneries or a stroll through the Ville Nouvelle.

Accommodation: Desert camp (Day 4), Fez riad (Day 5) · Meals: All meals included

Day 6: Fly to Nairobi, Kenya

Morning transfer to Fez airport for your flight to Nairobi (via Casablanca or other hub). Arrive in Kenya's capital in the afternoon. Transfer to your hotel in the Karen or Langata suburb. If time permits, visit the Giraffe Centre to hand-feed endangered Rothschild giraffes and the David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust to see orphaned baby elephants. Evening welcome dinner featuring Kenyan nyama choma (grilled meats) and local craft beers. Safari briefing and preparation for the days ahead.

Accommodation: Nairobi hotel · Meals: Dinner

Days 7–8: Masai Mara

Day 7: Fly or drive to the Masai Mara National Reserve. Afternoon game drive through Kenya's premier wildlife reserve, home to exceptional concentrations of predators: lions, cheetahs, leopards, and hyenas. During migration season (July-October), vast herds of wildebeest and zebra fill the plains in every direction. Sundowner drinks overlooking the Mara River.

Day 8: Dawn game drive to catch predators at their most active, followed by a Mara River crossing point where, with luck, you'll witness the dramatic spectacle of wildebeest braving crocodile-infested waters. Afternoon visit to a Maasai village to learn about their warrior traditions, cattle-herding culture, and the iconic jumping dance. Optional hot air balloon safari at sunrise followed by a champagne bush breakfast.

Accommodation: Masai Mara tented camp · Meals: All meals included

Days 9–10: Lake Nakuru & Transfer to Tanzania

Day 9: Drive north to Lake Nakuru, a soda lake in the Great Rift Valley famous for its flamingo spectacles and as a vital sanctuary for endangered black and white rhinos. The compact park delivers excellent game viewing: buffaloes, waterbuck, Rothschild giraffes, and over 400 bird species against the dramatic Rift Valley escarpment backdrop. A concentrated and photogenic wildlife experience.

Day 10: Cross the border into Tanzania, driving through Arusha and continuing to the edge of the Serengeti ecosystem. The landscape transforms from Kenyan highlands to Tanzanian plains, passing Maasai herders and acacia-studded savanna. Evening arrival at your Serengeti camp with a sunset over the endless plains that give the park its name (Siringet means "endless plains" in Maasai).

Accommodation: Lake Nakuru lodge (Day 9), Serengeti camp (Day 10) · Meals: All meals included

Days 11–13: Serengeti National Park

Three magnificent days in the Serengeti, Africa's most iconic safari destination. Day 11 explores the central Seronera Valley, a year-round wildlife hotspot where lions patrol the grasslands, leopards drape from kopje rocks, and hippos crowd the river pools. Day 12 ventures deeper into the park, following the migration herds or exploring the Western Corridor. Watch cheetahs hunt on the open plains and elephants silhouetted against the endless horizon. Day 13 begins with a dawn game drive to catch the bush at its most alive, followed by an afternoon visit to a Serengeti kopje for panoramic views. Optional balloon safari drifting silently over the plains at dawn. The Serengeti delivers moments that redefine your understanding of the natural world.

Accommodation: Serengeti tented camp or lodge · Meals: All meals included

Day 14: Ngorongoro Crater

Depart the Serengeti for the Ngorongoro Conservation Area. Descend 600 meters into the world's largest intact volcanic caldera, a natural amphitheater where approximately 25,000 large animals roam a mere 260 square kilometers. Black rhinos graze near Ngoitokitok Spring, massive bull elephants wander through the Lerai Forest, lions command the open grasslands, and flamingos streak the soda lake pink. The density of wildlife is extraordinary, and this is one of the few places in Tanzania where all Big Five can be spotted in a single day. Picnic lunch on the crater floor surrounded by this natural Eden.

Accommodation: Ngorongoro Crater rim lodge · Meals: All meals included

Days 15–16: Zanzibar

Day 15: Fly from Arusha to Zanzibar. Afternoon walking tour of Stone Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site: carved wooden doors, winding alleys, the House of Wonders, the Old Fort, and Freddie Mercury's birthplace. Feast on fresh seafood at Forodhani Gardens night market as the sun sets over the Indian Ocean.

Day 16: Choose your Zanzibar adventure. Relax on the powdery white sands of Nungwi or Kendwa Beach. Snorkel over coral reefs teeming with tropical fish. Take a spice tour through plantations of vanilla, clove, nutmeg, and cinnamon that earned Zanzibar its nickname, the Spice Island. Afternoon dhow sailing trip along the coast, watching fishermen bring in the day's catch as the afternoon light turns golden. Final island evening with a barefoot dinner on the beach.

Accommodation: Zanzibar beach resort · Meals: Breakfast daily, dinner on Day 15

Day 17: Fly to Cape Town, South Africa

Morning flight from Zanzibar to Cape Town (via Dar es Salaam or Nairobi). Arrive in the Mother City in the afternoon. Transfer to your hotel with views of Table Mountain. If weather is clear, take the revolving cable car to the summit for breathtaking 360-degree views over the city, harbor, Robben Island, and the Cape Flats. The flat-topped mountain is one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature. Evening seafood dinner at the V&A Waterfront as the mountain glows pink in the sunset.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel · Meals: Dinner

Day 18: Cape Peninsula & Winelands

Drive the spectacular Cape Peninsula: Chapman's Peak, Boulders Beach with its African penguin colony, and the dramatic Cape of Good Hope. Stop at a Simonstown cafe overlooking False Bay. Afternoon in the Cape Winelands: visit two or three estates in Stellenbosch and Franschhoek for tastings of Pinotage, Chenin Blanc, and Cap Classique sparkling wine. Gourmet lunch at a wine estate with mountain views. The contrast between the rugged coastline and the lush vineyard valleys showcases the extraordinary diversity of the Western Cape.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel or Winelands guesthouse · Meals: Breakfast & lunch

Day 19: Cape Town Culture & Robben Island

Morning ferry to Robben Island, where a former political prisoner guides you through the cell blocks that held Nelson Mandela for 18 years. Return to the mainland and explore the District Six Museum and the colorful Bo-Kaap neighborhood with its Cape Malay heritage. Afternoon at leisure: visit Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden, explore the Zeitz Museum of Contemporary Art Africa (MOCAA), or browse the Neighbourgoods Market. Farewell Cape Town dinner at a top restaurant.

Accommodation: Cape Town hotel · Meals: Breakfast

Days 20–21: Kruger National Park & Departure

Day 20: Fly from Cape Town to Kruger (approximately 2 hours via Johannesburg or direct to Nelspruit). Check into your safari lodge and enjoy an afternoon game drive as golden light draws animals to the waterholes. First Kruger sightings often include herds of elephants, giraffes, zebras, and impalas. Night drive to spot nocturnal animals: bush babies, civets, and leopards hunting under cover of darkness. Farewell bush dinner under the stars with sounds of the African night all around.

Day 21: Final dawn game drive, one last chance to spot the Big Five and say goodbye to the African bush. After breakfast, transfer to the airport for your departure flight. Carry three weeks of extraordinary memories: the silence of the Sahara, the thunder of the Great Migration, the turquoise waters of Zanzibar, and the roar of Kruger's lions. Africa has changed you forever.

Accommodation: Kruger safari lodge (Day 20) · Meals: All meals included (Day 20), Breakfast (Day 21)

Practical Information

Essential details for planning the ultimate multi-country Africa adventure.

Getting There & Around

International flights arrive at Marrakech (RAK). Internal flights connect Fez to Nairobi (via Casablanca), Arusha to Zanzibar, Zanzibar to Cape Town, and Cape Town to Kruger. This itinerary uses approximately 5 internal flights. Major airlines serving these routes include Royal Air Maroc, Kenya Airways, Precision Air, and South African Airways. Book internal flights well in advance, especially during peak season. Ground transfers by 4x4 safari vehicle cover all overland segments.

Visa Requirements

Morocco: visa-free for most nationalities (90 days). Kenya: e-visa required ($50) or East Africa Tourist Visa ($100). Tanzania: e-visa required ($50). South Africa: visa-free for most Western nationalities (90 days). Apply for all e-visas at least 2-4 weeks in advance. Passports must be valid for 6+ months with at least 4 blank pages for this multi-country trip. Carry printed copies of all visas and confirmations.

Health & Vaccinations

Yellow fever vaccination is mandatory for Kenya and Tanzania and required for South Africa entry when coming from endemic countries. Recommended: hepatitis A/B, typhoid, meningitis, and rabies. Anti-malaria prophylaxis is essential for Kenya, Tanzania, and Kruger. Morocco and Cape Town are malaria-free. Consult a travel health clinic 6-8 weeks before departure. Comprehensive travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage is absolutely essential for a trip of this scope.

Packing Essentials

Pack versatile layers suitable for desert, savanna, beach, and city. Neutral-colored safari clothing, a warm fleece for cool mornings, swimwear for Zanzibar and Cape Town, modest clothing for Morocco, comfortable walking shoes, and sandals. Essential gear: binoculars, camera with telephoto lens, universal power adapter, sunscreen SPF 50+, insect repellent with DEET, headlamp, and a lightweight daypack. Pack light as internal flights have weight limits (typically 15-20kg).

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Ultimate Africa itinerary.

Planning a multi-country Africa trip requires careful coordination of flights, visas, vaccinations, and seasonal timing. Start by choosing your priority regions and working with the optimal season (June-October is best for East African safaris). Book internal flights early as connections between African countries can be limited. Apply for all necessary visas well in advance. Ensure your vaccination records are up to date, especially yellow fever for East Africa. A 21-day trip like this one typically uses 4-5 internal flights to connect the countries efficiently. Working with an experienced tour operator or using a detailed itinerary like this one simplifies the logistics significantly.

Budget-conscious travelers staying in basic lodges and guesthouses can manage around $5,000-7,000 including internal flights. Mid-range travelers using comfortable lodges and tented camps should budget $8,000-12,000. Luxury travelers choosing premium safari lodges, boutique riads, and five-star hotels will spend $12,000-15,000 or more. International flights to and from Africa are additional. The biggest variable is accommodation in safari areas, where prices range dramatically from budget camping to ultra-luxury.

Morocco does not require specific vaccinations. South Africa requires a yellow fever certificate only if arriving from an endemic country (which Kenya and Tanzania are).
