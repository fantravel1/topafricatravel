Day-by-Day Itinerary

Your complete 10-day East Africa safari broken down day by day.

Day 1: Arrive in Nairobi

Arrive at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and transfer to your hotel in the city center or Karen suburb. If time permits, visit the Nairobi National Museum or the Karen Blixen Museum for a taste of Kenya's colonial history. In the afternoon, explore the Giraffe Centre in Langata where you can hand-feed endangered Rothschild giraffes. Evening welcome dinner at a local restaurant featuring Kenyan cuisine and a briefing about the safari ahead.

Accommodation: Nairobi hotel or guesthouse · Meals: Dinner

Days 2–3: Masai Mara National Reserve

Early morning departure for the Masai Mara, arriving in time for an afternoon game drive. The Mara is home to exceptional concentrations of lions, cheetahs, leopards, elephants, hippos, and during the Great Migration season (July-October), vast herds of wildebeest and zebra. On Day 3, enjoy a full-day game drive with picnic lunch in the bush. Optional early morning hot air balloon safari floating over the plains at dawn, followed by a champagne bush breakfast. Visit a Maasai village in the evening to learn about this iconic warrior culture, their traditions, and their harmonious relationship with wildlife.

Accommodation: Tented camp or lodge in the Mara · Meals: All meals included

Day 4: Lake Nakuru National Park

Drive north to Lake Nakuru, a soda lake famous for its spectacular flocks of flamingos and a critical sanctuary for endangered black and white rhinos. The compact park offers excellent game viewing with waterbuck, buffalo, Rothschild giraffe, baboons, and over 400 bird species. Circle the lake shore for flamingo photography and explore the surrounding acacia forest for leopard sightings. The dramatic backdrop of the Great Rift Valley escarpment makes this one of Kenya's most photogenic parks. Continue to the Kenya-Tanzania border or overnight near Nakuru.

Accommodation: Lake Nakuru lodge · Meals: All meals included

Days 5–7: Serengeti National Park

Cross the border into Tanzania and drive to the Serengeti, arriving at your camp in the central Seronera Valley. Over three magnificent days, explore the park's diverse ecosystems. Day 5 focuses on the Seronera area where resident predators hunt year-round along the river. Day 6 ventures deeper into the park following the migration herds or exploring the kopje-studded plains where lions and leopards bask. Day 7 offers a dawn game drive to catch predators at their most active, followed by an afternoon visit to a Serengeti hippo pool. The Serengeti's 14,763 square kilometers deliver constant surprises: cheetah hunts on the open plains, elephant herds silhouetted against the horizon, and sunsets that set the African sky ablaze.

Accommodation: Serengeti tented camp or lodge · Meals: All meals included

Day 8: Ngorongoro Crater

Depart the Serengeti at dawn and drive to the Ngorongoro Conservation Area. Descend 600 meters into the world's largest intact volcanic caldera, a natural amphitheater spanning 260 square kilometers and home to approximately 25,000 large animals. The crater floor offers astonishing density of wildlife: black rhinos graze near Ngoitokitok Spring, lions patrol the open grasslands, flamingos color the soda lakes pink, and elephants wander through the Lerai Forest. This is one of the few places in Tanzania where you can reliably spot all Big Five in a single day. Picnic lunch on the crater floor before ascending in the late afternoon.

Accommodation: Ngorongoro Crater rim lodge · Meals: All meals included

Days 9–10: Zanzibar

Fly from Arusha to Zanzibar, landing on the Spice Island by midday. Day 9 begins with a walking tour of Stone Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with winding alleys, carved wooden doors, and a rich Swahili, Arab, and European heritage. Visit the House of Wonders, the Old Fort, and the bustling Darajani Market. In the evening, feast on fresh seafood at the Forodhani Gardens night market. Day 10 is devoted to relaxation on Zanzibar's pristine white-sand beaches. Swim in turquoise waters, try snorkeling over coral reefs, or take an optional spice tour through nutmeg, clove, and vanilla plantations. Transfer to the airport for your departure flight with memories of an extraordinary African journey.

Accommodation: Zanzibar beach resort or boutique hotel · Meals: Breakfast & dinner

Practical Information

Everything you need to know to prepare for your East Africa safari.

Getting There & Around

International flights arrive at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (NBO) in Nairobi. The safari continues overland by 4x4 vehicle through Kenya and into Tanzania. Internal flights connect Arusha to Zanzibar (approximately 1.5 hours). Some itineraries use bush flights within the Serengeti. Road transfers between parks take 4-6 hours through stunning Great Rift Valley scenery.

Visa & Documents

Most nationalities need visas for both Kenya and Tanzania. The East Africa Tourist Visa ($100) covers Kenya, Uganda, and Rwanda. Tanzania e-visas cost $50 for most nationalities. Apply online in advance. Your passport must be valid for 6+ months with at least 2 blank pages. A yellow fever vaccination certificate is required if traveling from an endemic area.

What to Pack

Pack neutral-colored clothing (khaki, olive, tan), layering pieces for cool mornings and warm afternoons, a wide-brimmed hat, quality sunglasses, sunscreen SPF 50+, insect repellent, binoculars, a camera with telephoto lens, and a headlamp or flashlight. A warm fleece or jacket is essential for early morning game drives and the cool Ngorongoro Crater rim.

Health & Safety

Consult a travel health clinic 6-8 weeks before departure. Recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A/B, typhoid, and yellow fever. Anti-malaria prophylaxis is strongly advised for all areas on this itinerary. Bring a basic first-aid kit. Always follow your safari guide's instructions around wildlife. Travel insurance covering medical evacuation is essential for East Africa safaris.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Classic East Africa Safari itinerary.

The best time for this East Africa safari itinerary is during the dry season from June to October. This is when the Great Migration reaches the Masai Mara (July-October) and wildlife concentrates around water sources in both Kenya and Tanzania, making game viewing exceptional. The Serengeti offers excellent viewing year-round, but river crossings peak from July to September. January to March is also excellent for calving season in the southern Serengeti.

A 10-day East Africa safari typically costs between $2,000 and $5,000 per person, depending on accommodation level and season. Budget options with camping and basic lodges start around $2,000. Mid-range safaris with comfortable tented camps run $3,000-4,000. Luxury safaris with premium lodges and private conservancies can reach $5,000 or more. Costs generally include accommodation, meals, game drives, park fees, and internal transfers, but international flights are usually extra.

Most nationalities require visas for both Kenya and Tanzania. The East Africa Tourist Visa covers Kenya, Uganda, and Rwanda for $100 and is valid for 90 days. Tanzania offers a visa on arrival or e-visa for $50 for most nationalities. We recommend applying for e-visas in advance to avoid delays at the border. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your travel dates with at least 2 blank pages.
