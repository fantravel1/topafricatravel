Africa Travel ItinerariesCurated Multi-Day Adventures

Expert-crafted itineraries spanning the continent's most extraordinary destinations. From 8-day escapes to 21-day odysseys, every journey is designed to reveal Africa's true depth and beauty.

Featured Itineraries

Six curated journeys across Africa, each designed by travel experts to deliver unforgettable experiences.

Classic East Africa Safari

10 Days · Kenya & Tanzania · Difficulty: Easy to Moderate

The quintessential African safari spanning two legendary wildlife destinations.

  • Days 1-2: Arrive in Nairobi, city tour, Nairobi National Museum, Karen Blixen Museum
  • Days 3-4: Masai Mara National Reserve — Big Five game drives, Mara River, Maasai village visit
  • Days 5-6: Cross into Tanzania, Serengeti National Park — endless plains, wildebeest migration
  • Day 7: Ngorongoro Crater — the world's largest intact caldera, dense wildlife
  • Days 8-10: Fly to Zanzibar — Stone Town, spice tours, pristine Indian Ocean beaches, departure

Morocco Discovery

8 Days · Morocco · Difficulty: Moderate

A journey through imperial cities, mountain passes, and Saharan dunes.

  • Days 1-2: Marrakech — Jemaa el-Fnaa, souks, Majorelle Garden, Bahia Palace, hammam
  • Day 3: Atlas Mountains — Berber villages, Toubkal foothills, traditional lunch
  • Days 4-5: Sahara Desert via Aït Benhaddou — camel trek, desert camp, stargazing at Erg Chebbi
  • Day 6: Fez — medieval medina, tanneries, Al Quaraouiyine University, artisan workshops
  • Days 7-8: Chefchaouen — the Blue Pearl, Rif Mountains, local cuisine, departure from Tangier

South Africa Highlights

12 Days · South Africa · Difficulty: Easy

From cosmopolitan cities to wildlife reserves and vineyard-covered valleys.

  • Days 1-3: Cape Town — Table Mountain, V&A Waterfront, Robben Island, Bo-Kaap, Cape Point
  • Days 4-5: Winelands — Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, wine tastings, gourmet dining
  • Days 6-8: Garden Route — Hermanus whale watching, Knysna, Tsitsikamma, Plettenberg Bay
  • Days 9-11: Kruger National Park — Big Five game drives, bush walks, sunset safaris
  • Day 12: Johannesburg — Apartheid Museum, Soweto, Constitution Hill, departure

West Africa Heritage

14 Days · Ghana & Senegal · Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging

A deep cultural immersion into West Africa's history, music, art, and resilience.

  • Days 1-3: Accra, Ghana — Jamestown, Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum, Makola Market, vibrant nightlife
  • Days 4-5: Cape Coast & Elmina — slave castles, Kakum National Park canopy walk, fishing villages
  • Days 6-7: Kumasi — Ashanti Kingdom capital, Manhyia Palace, Kejetia Market, kente weaving
  • Days 8-10: Dakar, Senegal — African Renaissance Monument, IFAN Museum, Ngor Island, Senegalese cuisine
  • Days 11-12: Gorée Island & Lac Rose — House of Slaves, pink lake, historical reflection
  • Days 13-14: Saint-Louis — UNESCO colonial architecture, Djoudj bird sanctuary, Lompoul Desert, departure

Egypt & The Nile

9 Days · Egypt · Difficulty: Easy

Five thousand years of civilization along the world's longest river.

  • Days 1-2: Cairo — Pyramids of Giza, Sphinx, Egyptian Museum, Khan el-Khalili bazaar, Coptic Cairo
  • Day 3: Memphis & Saqqara — Step Pyramid, ancient capital, palm groves
  • Days 4-5: Luxor — Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple, Hatshepsut Temple, Luxor Temple at sunset
  • Days 6-7: Nile Cruise — Edfu, Kom Ombo, felucca sailing, riverside villages, Aswan, Philae Temple
  • Days 8-9: Red Sea coast — Hurghada, coral reef snorkeling, desert excursion, departure

Ultimate Africa

21 Days · Morocco, Kenya, Tanzania & South Africa · Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging

The definitive continent-spanning journey covering North, East, and Southern Africa.

  • Days 1-4: Morocco — Marrakech medina, Atlas Mountains, Sahara desert camp, Fez
  • Days 5-8: Kenya — Nairobi, Masai Mara game drives, Lake Nakuru flamingos, Great Rift Valley
  • Days 9-13: Tanzania — Serengeti, Ngorongoro Crater, Tarangire elephants, Zanzibar beaches
  • Days 14-17: South Africa — Cape Town, Table Mountain, Cape Winelands, Garden Route
  • Days 18-21: Kruger National Park — Big Five safaris, bush camp, Johannesburg, Soweto, departure

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning your Africa itinerary.

What is the best time to visit Africa for a safari?

The best time for East African safaris is during the dry season from June to October, when wildlife gathers around water sources. Southern Africa is best visited from May to September. However, Africa is vast and each region has its own optimal seasons, so the best time depends on your specific itinerary and destinations.

How far in advance should I plan an Africa trip?

We recommend planning your Africa trip 6 to 12 months in advance, especially for peak season safaris and popular destinations. This allows time for visa processing, securing accommodations in sought-after lodges, and booking internal flights. Last-minute trips are possible but offer fewer options.

Can I combine multiple countries in one Africa trip?

Absolutely. Multi-country itineraries are among the most rewarding ways to experience Africa. Popular combinations include Kenya and Tanzania for East African safaris, Morocco with Egypt for North Africa, and South Africa with Mozambique or Botswana for southern Africa. Our curated itineraries are designed for seamless multi-country travel.

What difficulty levels do your itineraries include?

Our itineraries range from Easy (suitable for all fitness levels, mostly vehicle-based with comfortable accommodations) to Moderate (some walking, varied accommodation types) to Challenging (significant physical activity, remote areas, basic facilities). Each itinerary clearly states its difficulty level so you can choose the right adventure for you.
